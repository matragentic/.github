# Code of Conduct

## The spirit of this document

Most codes of conduct are written to prevent the worst. This one is written to describe the best — the kind of community we are trying to build — and to name clearly what falls outside it.

Matragentic is a project built on matriarchal principles: distributed authority, structural care, reconciliation over adjudication, and the belief that a community has obligations to its members, not only the reverse. This code of conduct is an expression of those principles applied to everyone who participates in the project — contributors, maintainers, users, and visitors.

---

## What we ask of each other

**Generosity of interpretation.** Assume the person you are speaking with is acting in good faith, even when you disagree with them. Especially when you disagree with them. Text is a limited medium; much of what reads as hostility is actually uncertainty, frustration, or cultural difference.

**Honesty without cruelty.** Say what you actually think. Disagree when you disagree. Name problems when you see them. But do so in a way that treats the other person as someone whose experience and perspective have value, even if their current contribution does not.

**Care for the whole, not just your part.** Notice when someone is struggling. Acknowledge good work. Ask questions before making assumptions about someone's intent. The health of the community is everyone's concern, not a role reserved for maintainers.

**Slowness where it matters.** Not every disagreement needs resolving in the same thread. Not every decision needs making today. Some of the best outcomes come from stepping away, thinking, and returning with more clarity. We value considered responses over fast ones.

**Willingness to be changed.** If you enter a conversation certain you are right and leave certain you are right, the conversation did not happen. Genuine participation means holding your position firmly enough to articulate it and loosely enough to revise it.

---

## How we handle disagreement

Disagreement is welcome here. It is signal, not noise. A project that suppresses disagreement produces shallow consensus; a project that handles disagreement well produces genuine understanding.

Our approach mirrors the project's own conflict resolution design:

**Start with direct dialogue.** If you disagree with someone, speak to them — in the thread, in an issue, in a direct message if appropriate. State your position, your underlying concern, and what you think the other person might be right about. Most disagreements resolve here.

**Invite reflection if needed.** If direct dialogue is not resolving, ask a maintainer to facilitate. Their role is not to decide who is right but to help both parties understand each other and find a path forward. Multiple paths may be offered; the choice remains with those involved.

**Accept that some disagreements persist.** Not every difference of view resolves into agreement. Sometimes the honest outcome is "we see this differently, and here is how we will work together despite that." This is a valid and respected outcome.

---

## What is not welcome

Some behaviours are not matters of interpretation or disagreement. They are simply outside what this community will hold.

**Harassment** in any form — sustained unwanted contact, threats, intimidation, doxxing, or deliberate attempts to make someone feel unsafe or unwelcome.

**Discrimination** based on race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, nationality, or any other characteristic. This includes slurs, stereotyping, and exclusionary language, whether or not it is intended as humour.

**Sexualised language or attention** in project spaces. This is a professional and intellectual community; conduct yourself accordingly.

**Deliberate disruption** — derailing discussions, spamming, trolling, or persistent bad-faith engagement designed to waste others' time or energy.

**Retaliation** against anyone who reports a concern or participates in resolving one.

**Publishing others' private information** without explicit permission.

## When something goes wrong

If you experience or witness behaviour that falls outside this code of conduct, or if you are unsure whether something crosses a line, you are welcome to raise it.

**For immediate concerns:** contact the project maintainers at conduct@matragentic.org. Reports are handled confidentially. You will receive an acknowledgement within 48 hours and a substantive response within one week.

**For concerns about a maintainer:** contact a different maintainer directly, or use the same email address — reports about maintainers are handled by the remaining maintainers, not by the person named.

**What happens next:** the maintainers will listen, gather context where needed, and respond. Responses may range from a private conversation to a temporary or permanent ban from project spaces, depending on the severity and pattern of the behaviour. The goal is always to restore the community's health, not to punish.

We will not disclose the identity of anyone who reports a concern without their explicit permission.

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces — repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, any chat or forum associated with the project, and events held under the project's name. It also applies when someone is representing the project in public spaces.

---

## A note on the project's character

Matragentic is a project about care, rest, and the conditions that make sustained collective work possible. We take that seriously in how we build the project itself, not only in what the project builds.

This means we will sometimes be slower than other projects. We will sometimes pause to tend to a community concern rather than shipping a feature. We will sometimes choose the response that strengthens a relationship over the one that resolves an issue fastest. These are not inefficiencies. They are expressions of what the project believes.

If that resonates with you, you are welcome here.
